Meet Illinois' "It could be worse" bipartisan campaign donor class

Politics

By LGIS News Service | Aug 23, 2018


Illinois Republican Governor Bruce Rauner and Chicago Democrat Mayor Rahm Emanuel share more than 320 of the same campaign donors. | Scapi Magazine

Some 323 campaign donors to Democrat Chicago Mayor Rahm Emanuel have also given to his friend-turned-nemesis, Republican Illinois Gov. Bruce Rauner, according to an analysis of Illinois State Board of Elections (ISBE) records by Chicago City Wire.

The list features some of Chicago's wealthiest families, and most politically connected individuals and companies, including lawyers, traders, asset managers and real estate developers.

Partners at Chicago-based law firm Kirkland & Ellis feature most prominently, with the list including 27 partners reporting a collective $946,975 in donations to Emanuel and Rauner.

Leading the list-- firm Chairman Jeffrey C. Hammes of Glencoe, and his wife, Linda, who have given Rauner $60,000 and Emanuel $140,800.

Hammes, who ISBE records show voted in the 2014 Republican primary and the 2012 Democrat one, recently bought a $15 million home in Beverly Hills, Calif, according to published reports.

Kirkland partner Matthew Steinmetz of Lincoln Park gave Rauner $50,300 and Emanuel $60,400, ISBE records show.

Steinmetz, a 1987 graduate of the University of Notre Dame Law School, "focuses primarily on leveraged buyouts of public and private companies, as well as recaps and joint ventures," per his corporate biography.

Other $50,000-plus Kirkland donors are Ted Zook of Winnetka ($35,300 to Rauner/$41,400 to Emanuel); Patrick Nash of Lincoln Park ($10,000/$70,400); Richard Campbell of Glenview ($55,300/$25,000); and James Sprayregen ($20,000/$70,400), Jack Levin ($75,000/$40,500) and Stephen Ritchie ($50,000/$51,000) of Glencoe.

Other notable donors to Emanuel and Rauner include:

Citadel CEO and founder Ken Griffin, who is Illinois' richest resident with a reported net worth of $8.5 billion. He has been Rauner's biggest backer, giving his campaign more than $36 million. Griffin has given Emanuel $330,300.

Private equity investor Donald Edwards of Lincoln Park and his wife, Anne. They have given Rauner $360,600 and Emanuel $445,400.

Edwards worked with Rauner at GTCR from 1994 to 2005.

Seven members of the Crown family, including Henry Crown; company Chairman Lester Crown; and two of his sons, company President James Crown ($30,300 to Rauner/$156,800 to Emanuel) and A. Steven Crown ($30,000/$60,000).

Groupon Founder Eric Lefkofsky of Glencoe and his wife, Liz, have given Rauner $135,600 and Emanuel $271,600.

Private equity investor Byron Trott of Winnetka, once called Warren Buffett's favorite banker when he ran Chicago's Goldman Sachs office, has given Rauner $130,300 and Emanuel $66,800. His former boss, one-time Goldman CEO and U.S. Treasury Secretary Hank Paulson of Barrington also made the list, giving Rauner $80,300 and Emanuel $70,200.

Former Exelon Chairman and CEO John Rowe of Chicago has given Rauner $65,300 and Emanuel $56,800. Current Exelon CEO Chris Crane of St. Charles, his wife Amy and Exelon company public affairs executive William Von Hoene also gave to both Rauner and Emanuel.

Chicago White Sox and Bulls Chairman Jerry Reinsdorf of Chicago has given Rauner $58,000 and Emanuel $77,500.

Morningstar founder and CEO Joseph Mansueto of Chicago has given Rauner $205,300 and Emanuel $200,000.

Apple Vacations CEO Timothy Mullen of Chicago has given Rauner $15,300 and Emanuel $215,900.

David Helfand, co-president of Sam Zell's Equity Group Investments, gave Rauner $55,300 and Emanuel $135,300.
