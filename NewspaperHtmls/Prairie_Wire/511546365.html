Analysis: Northwestern University football revenue per player ranks 36th in nation

Sports

By LGIS Newsfeed | Sep 14, 2018

Northwestern University football earns an average of $375,549 per player annually – the 36th highest rate in the nation – according to a Prairie State Wire analysis of federal education data.

The analysis found that the university had 103 students playing on its football team and took in a total of $38,681,564 in total football revenues.

The other Illinois school among the the nation’s 100 colleges and universities that take in the most football revenue was University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (No. 43). Northern Illinois University ranked No. 116, and Southern Illinois University-Carbondale came in at No. 178. 

The U.S. Department of Education’s office of postsecondary education collects athletics data annually from all institutions with intercollegiate athletics programs that receive funding through federal financial aid programs. Data for 2016 – the latest year available – includes more than 2,000 schools nationwide.
