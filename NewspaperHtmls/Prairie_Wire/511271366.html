CORRECTION Analysis: At top-scoring school in the state, 100% passed annual tests

Schools

By LGIS Newsfeed | Nov 16, 2017

At Northside College Preparatory High School, 100 percent of students scored well enough on this year’s annual state assessments to be considered ready for the next level, making it the top-performing school in the state, according to a Prairie State Wire analysis of Illinois State Board of Education data.

Of the 3,796 schools in the state, many saw high percentages of their students fall below standards on the annual tests.

Statewide, 34 percent of students met or exceeded expectations. The remaining 66 percent did not meet, partially met or approached expectations.

The Partnership for Assessment of Readiness for College and Careers assesses a student’s aptitude in math and English language arts and is based on the Common Core State Standard principles. In 2016, the test was administered to students in elementary to junior level of high school. Illinois is one of eight states participating in PARCC as a means to measure a student’s preparedness for higher education.

The scores – a composite derived from the combined results of the Math and English tests – reveals whether a student is or is not ready for the next level. Student scores either “did not meet,” “partially met,” “approach,” “met” or “exceeded” expectations. Any student who meets or exceeds expectations is considered ready for the next level.
