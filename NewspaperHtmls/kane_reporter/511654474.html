Analysis: Average Geneva School District 304 teacher comp is $125K

Schools

By LGIS News Service | Dec 5, 2018


Geneva Unit District 304 teachers are striking. They currently earn an average of $125,000 for nine months of work. | Youtube

The average teacher at Geneva Unit District 304 received $124,673 in total compensation in 2017 for working nine months, according to an analysis of Illinois State Board of Education (ISBE) and Teachers Retirement System (TRS) records by the Kane County Reporter.

The analysis covers 483 district 304 employees who received a collective $60.2 million in 2017.

Its total compensation number is calculated by combining, for each employee, their base salary for nine months, cost of benefits (at 30 percent), paid sick days and their taxpayer-funded pension contribution. 

The pension contribution was 37 percent of each employee's base salary in 2017, according to TRS.

Public school employees pay in to the state pension system approximately 3 percent of what they stand to collect in retirement, or $30,000 for each $1 million received. Taxpayers make up the difference.

Of the employee pay packages analyzed, 316 of the 483 (65 percent) received more than $100,000; 28 earned more than $200,000, including top-compensated principal Thomas Rogers ($330,181) and 18 teachers.

Geneva teachers went on strike Tuesday, demanding a salary increase of 18 percent, or 6 percent for each of the next three years.
