7.8 percent of borrowers default on student loans in Illinois

Schools

By Kane County Times reports | Oct 15, 2018

Aurora University was among four-year schools in Illinois to report the highest rates of borrowers who started repaying student loans in 2014 and defaulted on those loans by the end of 2016, according to the latest data from the U.S. Department of Education.

Ninety-two institutions reported 160,187 students entered repayment on student loans. And 12,480 – or 7.8 percent – defaulted on those loans.

The data includes students at four-year schools receiving federal student aid who entered repayment on selected federal loans in 2014 and defaulted before the end of the second fiscal year.
