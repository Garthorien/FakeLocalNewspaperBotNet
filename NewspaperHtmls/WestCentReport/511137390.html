Chester-East Lincoln CCSD 61 holds $2.5 million in long-term debt

Schools

By Local Labs News Service | Jul 11, 2017

Chester-East Lincoln Community Consolidated School District 61 has $2,505,000 in debt, according to Illinois State Board of Education 2016 financial reports obtained by the West Central Reporter.

Because state law allows the district to borrow a total of $5,091,728, Chester-East Lincoln CCSD 61 has reached 49.2 percent of its debt capacity.

The state limits how much school districts can borrow, capping their debt at a percentage of the district's property wealth, as determined by county and township assessors.

Community unit school districts are able to borrow up to 13.8 percent of their total property value, or Equalized Assessed Value (EAV). Elementary school districts are able to borrow up to 6.9 percent of their EAV.

However, school districts exceeding these debt caps are not punished or sanctioned, as the state has no enforcement mechanism in place.

In 2015, Chester-East Lincoln 61, an elementary school district, had an EAV of $73,793,159, according to ISBE.
